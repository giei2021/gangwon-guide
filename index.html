Geographic Features

The Taebaek Mountain Range divides Gangwon into two distinct zones with different climates and lifestyles:

🏔

Yeongso (Western / Inland)

Chuncheon, Wonju, Hongcheon, Pyeongchang, Cheorwon — continental climate, hot summers, cold winters

🌊

Yeongdong (Eastern / Coastal)

Gangneung, Sokcho, Donghae, Samcheok, Goseong, Yangyang — East Sea influence, milder winters, higher rainfall

Gangwon hosts South Korea's highest mountains and three national parks (Seoraksan, Odaesan, Chiaksan).

Pre-Arrival Checklist for New NATs

  1. Passport photos — Must meet Korean immigration office requirements. View requirements (PDF)
  2. Sufficient cash — Bring enough for the first month before your settlement allowance arrives. Also need KRW 35,000 cash for the Alien Registration Card (ARC) application fee.
  3. Passport copies — Several copies of both your bio page and Korean visa page.
  4. Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) — Issued by your home country's tax authority. Required to open a Korean bank account.
  5. Apostilled driver's license — Useful for converting to a Korean license. Korean license conversion info
  6. Certificate of Residency — If from Australia, Ireland, New Zealand, South Africa, UK, or USA: apply for income tax exemption (first 2 years). Obtain well in advance.
  7. Temporary SIM / connectivity — ARC takes ~2 weeks; prepare a phone solution: EG Sim or Korea SIM

Regional Characteristics & Living Info

💡 Good to know
Even from the most rural placements, Seoul is always accessible: ~45-min bus to Chuncheon, then subway into Seoul. Total ~2 hours, ~$6–7 USD. Most NETs visit Seoul on weekends with ease.

City Highlights

Chuncheon (춘천) — Provincial Capital

  • Dakgalbi Street / Myeongdong Street: Dozens of restaurants serving Chuncheon's famous spicy stir-fried chicken
  • Makguksu: Cold buckwheat noodles — a Chuncheon specialty
  • Nami Island (남이섬): Iconic four-seasons island reached by a 5-min ferry; K-drama filming location — namisum.com/en
  • University town: Hallym University, Kangwon National University — expat community present
  • Access to Seoul: ~45-min express bus → total ~2 hours, ~$6–7 USD

Gangneung (강릉) — East Coast Hub

  • 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics ice events host city
  • Gangneung Coffee Street (안목해변): South Korea's coffee culture capital — hundreds of specialty cafes
  • KTX: Seoul Station → Gangneung in ~2 hours
  • Chodang Tofu (초당두부): Extra-silky tofu made with East Sea seawater
  • Ojukheon (오죽헌): Birthplace of scholar Yi I; UNESCO Tentative World Heritage
  • Tourist Info Center: English/Japanese/Chinese assistance ☎ +82-33-640-4534

Sokcho (속초) — Gateway to Seoraksan

  • Entry point for Seoraksan National Park — famous for spring wildflowers and autumn foliage
  • Abai Village (아바이마을): North Korean refugee cultural village; famous Abai stuffed squid sausage
  • Sokcho Tourist & Fishery Market: Snow crab (홍게), fresh seafood, squid dishes

Hwacheon (화천) — Rural Mountain Town

  • Hwacheon Sancheoneo Ice Fishing Festival (January): One of the world's top 4 winter festivals
  • Peaceful rural environment; ideal for those who enjoy nature and a slower pace of life
  • Short bus to Chuncheon for city conveniences

Pyeongchang (평창) — 2018 Winter Olympics Host

  • Host of the 2018 Pyeongchang Winter Olympics
  • Yongpyong Resort: South Korea's premier ski resort; 2018 Olympic alpine ski venue
  • Odaesan National Park: Woljeongsa Temple fir-tree forest path — one of Korea's most beautiful nature walks
  • Daegwallyeong: Highland plateau (~800m); Samyang Ranch, pastoral scenery, sheep farms

Yangyang (양양) — Surfing Capital

  • Jukdo & Ingu Beaches: Korea's premier surfing spots — significant international community
  • Naksan Temple (낙산사): Famous east coast Buddhist temple overlooking the sea
  • Yangyang International Airport: International flight connections available

Climate & Weather

🏔

Yeongso (Western) Zone

Chuncheon, Wonju, Hongcheon, Cheorwon
Continental: hot summers, very cold winters, large daily temperature swings

🌊

Yeongdong (Coastal) Zone

Gangneung, Sokcho, Donghae, Samcheok, Goseong, Yangyang
East Sea influence: milder winters (~2°C warmer), higher rainfall

Seasonal Guide

🌸
Spring
Mar – May

Cherry blossoms and azaleas. Temperature swings are large — carry a light jacket. Best hiking by late April.

☀
Summer
Jun – Aug

East coast beach season; surfing in Yangyang; Hongcheon River rafting. Be aware of monsoon (jangma) and typhoons.

🍂
Autumn
Sep – Nov

Ideal outdoor season. Seoraksan autumn foliage peaks early-to-mid October. Best season for hiking.

❄
Winter
Dec – Feb

Ski season. Heavy snowfall especially in Daegwallyeong. Pack thermal layers, waterproof boots, and a heavy coat.

🔄 Practical Food Tips
A full restaurant meal typically costs KRW 8,000–15,000 (~$6–11 USD) — far cheaper and often higher quality than comparable meals abroad. Convenience stores (CU, GS25, 7-Eleven) are open 24 hours with excellent ready-to-eat options. Delivery apps Baemin (배달의민족) and Coupang Eats are widely used, even in smaller towns.

Key Takeaways from NET Testimonials

💬 What Current NETs Say
  • Even rural towns are only ~2 hours from Seoul — most NETs visit on weekends (~$6–7 USD)
  • Food is affordable and excellent — full meals $5–8, far cheaper than Western countries
  • Rural placements offer a peaceful, community-oriented lifestyle with mountain views
  • Free gym facilities are often available in small towns
  • Korean co-workers frequently show warmth through food — accept meal invitations warmly
  • Work-life balance as an EPIK teacher is generally strong
